Modifications

Modifications are where you really get to let your car shine. These modifications will improve vehicle performance and the driver's experience. Almost all of these modifications can be done by anyone, but the last one is much more involved but also yields the largest gain in performance. Any of these modifications will set a project car apart from a stock car. this carousel allows you to view the benefits and our rating on difficulty of installing the components that make up the modification. Each section will include a list of links to reputable manufacturers/sellers of parts suited for such modifications.

Intake

Intake modifications are the easiest to install on a project car. They usually involve undoing very few bolts and then removing the old intake and replacing it with a new performance header. Intake headers will not yield more than a 5 horsepower gain, but are an important prerequisite if you plan to modify your vehicle with a forced induction kit.

  • Injen
  • K&N
  • AEM

Suspension

Suspension modifications change how your project car handles the road. Better suspension will reduce body torsion as well as increase road grip on tighter corners. Suspension is moderately difficult to install because it requires the vehicle to be jacked up. It is also difficult because suspension component bolts are very tight and there are many of them that will need to be removed and reinstalled once the new suspension kit is installed.

  • KW Suspensions
  • Bilstien
  • Eibach

Brakes

Brake modifications are simple and improve how the car feels when braking. The car will feel much more effective in braking which is very useful for more expressive driving, such as drifting. Brake modifications are moderately difficult due to very heavily torqued bolts on the brake calipers, and the inherent risks of performing brake modifications incorrectly.

  • Brembo
  • Wilwood
  • Bosch

Exhaust

Exhaust modifications are what make a project car sound like a race car. Exhaust modifications turn heads if done right. Improper or cheap exhaust modifications make a car sound like it is in a state of disrepair rather than a speed demon. exhaust modifications can also improve performance of your car due to an increase in the flow of exhaust gasses in a wider diameter exhaust pipe. exhaust modifications are relatively difficult due to the fact that you have to be under the vehicle while installing the exhaust system, and the size of the exhaust pipes you will install will determine the difficulty of fitment and may require fabrication of custom parts. Exhaust modifications are a good thing to perform if you are considering implementing a forced induction modification.

  • Borla
  • FlowMaster
  • Crown

Forced induction

Forced induction is the defacto way to improve performance of your vehicle. Forced induction has the greatest risk of damaging your vehicles engine and transmission, and should only be done if you are very confident in your abilities to perform the modification. Forced induction works by packing more air into the engine block before combustion, allowing the fuel system to pump more fuel and make more power. forced induction in small amounts is relatively safe with an otherwise stock vehicle, but more performant modifications will require upgrades to the fuel system, the intake, the exhaust, and the transmission to ensure that your vehicle can handle the extra power it is making. this modification is extremely difficult and is not for the faint of heart, so if you can do it you should should be very proud of yourself.

  • Garett
  • CX
  • Prescision turbo
